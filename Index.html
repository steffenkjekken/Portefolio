Community Science Museum

Semester Project 1

Link to project

Brief:

A interactive science museum, aiming to attract children (7-15) & their families. The site must be informative & appealing, without pandering, it should take for granted that the audience is inquisitive & intelligent. A responsive site that would attract people to visit the museum.

Process:

Began with researching how other Museums had approached making their site. Bright colors, large images & "exiting" fonts were the running theme. Made a design that i thought fitted the criteria and target audience. Coded the site in HTML, and styled in CSS with the mobile-first approach.

Reflection:

A site im very satisfied with, both from a design and coding perspective. The site looks great on both mobile and desktop. Here i began to understand css-grid, wich helped me alot. I also think that some basic animations would make the site a bit more "playfull".

Futune

Project Exam 1

Link to project

Brief:

Building a blog-site, using data from Wordpress API. Futune is a platform to discover music, albums & artists. This spesific "page" was displaying the work of the artist; Kanye West. The target group is mainly young people and young adults interrested in music.

Process

The design is simple and minimalistic, and rely on large typography. I wanted a retro feeling, without too many images or colors. Made a logo too define the identity of "Futune". Also made multiple animation details that made the site more lively. The blog post are written in wordpress and fetched to the site using javascript. The data can be viewed in a "carousell" on the front page and also on a specific-post page.

Reflection:

I think i found a good balance in my design being not too boring nor being too much. First time using typography too this extent for design objects. Some places i should have used the text-shadow property instead of duplicating text. I learned alot javascript from this project.
